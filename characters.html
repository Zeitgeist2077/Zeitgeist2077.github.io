Primarchs

Roboute Guilliman

Ultramarines

Revived in the 41st millennium, Guilliman now rules as Lord Commander of the Imperium. A master of logistics and strategy, he struggles to hold together a decaying empire built on faith rather than reason.

Lion El'Jonson

Dark Angels

Recently awakened after ten thousand years, the Lion has returned as a relentless hunter of Chaos, leading secretive campaigns across the Imperium Nihilus.

Leman Russ

Space Wolves

The savage king of Fenris disappeared into the Eye of Terror, seeking a way to save the Emperor. Legends say he will return in the Imperium’s darkest hour.

Rogal Dorn

Imperial Fists

The Emperor’s praetorian and master of defence, Dorn vanished after the Heresy, leaving behind only his hand as proof of his fate.

Sanguinius

Blood Angels

Beloved angelic Primarch who foresaw his own death. He faced Horus alone during the Siege of Terra and was slain, becoming a symbol of sacrifice.

Vulkan

Salamanders

A perpetual who cannot truly die, Vulkan values humanity above all. He vanished after the Heresy but is destined to return when his artefacts are found.

Corvus Corax

Raven Guard

After the Heresy, Corax entered the Warp and became a shadow-like being, hunting traitors in the darkness.

Jaghatai Khan

White Scars

The master of speed and hit-and-run warfare vanished into the Webway while chasing Dark Eldar raiders.

Ferrus Manus

Iron Hands

Killed by Fulgrim early in the Heresy, Ferrus Manus’ death hardened his Legion into cold, machine-obsessed warriors.

Horus

Warmaster

The Emperor’s favoured son turned traitor, Horus led a galaxy-wide rebellion before being slain by the Emperor aboard his flagship.

Fulgrim

Slaanesh

Obsessed with perfection, Fulgrim fell to Slaanesh and became a Daemon Primarch embodying excess and corruption.

Perturabo

Iron Warriors

A bitter genius of siege warfare, Perturabo turned against the Emperor and now rules as a Daemon Prince of Chaos.

Angron

Khorne

Driven mad by the Butcher’s Nails, Angron became a Daemon Primarch of Khorne, living only for rage and slaughter.

Mortarion

Nurgle

Enduring endless suffering, Mortarion embraced Nurgle to save his Legion, becoming a plague-ridden Daemon Primarch.

Magnus the Red

Tzeentch

A tragic figure, Magnus’ attempt to warn the Emperor led to his fall. He now serves Tzeentch as a master of sorcery.

Konrad Curze

Night Lords

Haunted by visions of the future, Curze embraced terror as justice and ultimately allowed himself to be assassinated.

Lorgar

Word Bearers

The first to worship Chaos, Lorgar’s religious zeal helped ignite the Horus Heresy.

Alpharius / Omegon

Alpha Legion

Twin Primarchs of secrecy and deception. Their true loyalties and fate remain one of the galaxy’s greatest mysteries.

Chaos Gods

Khorne

God of War

Khorne is the Chaos God of rage, war, and bloodshed. He cares not from where the blood flows, only that it does.

Tzeentch

God of Change

Master of fate, magic, and manipulation, Tzeentch weaves endless schemes across time.

Nurgle

God of Decay

The Plague God embodies disease and entropy, yet also twisted forms of resilience and rebirth.

Slaanesh

God of Excess

Slaanesh represents pleasure, obsession, and excess in all forms, corrupting those who seek perfection.
